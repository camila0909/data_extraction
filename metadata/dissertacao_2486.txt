ABSTRACT

This dissertation exposes an application of linear algebra, the Leslie
model, which describes the population growth of females of a given
population. For this, the deﬁnition of eigenvalues, eigenvectors and
diagonalization of matrices is presented, aiming the structuring of the
model. With the purpose of showing the students of basic education
a practical example involving matrices, this work proposes to use the
Leslie model in a study of the future female projection distributed in
age groups in order to verify the sustainability of the Brazilian social
security system in the long term.

Keywords: Linear algebra. Leslie’s model. Population growth. Ma-
trices.
