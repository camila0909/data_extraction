ABSTRACT

The present work sought to investigate the understanding expressed by high school stu-

dents regarding the resolution of counting problems involving the Drawer Principle and
Kaplansky’s Slogans. Currently, such subjects are not part of the Basic Education Mathe-

matics curriculum. However, in this investigative process, we argue that the study of these

themes, during high school, is possible. Since the basic concepts and / or prerequisites

for the study of the Principle of Drawers and Kaplansky’s Slogans are present among
the contents studied in Basic Education (basic arithmetic and basic counting tools, res-

pectively). The research involved the participation of students in the 3rd grade of high

school who study at a full-time school in the state of CearÆ. For that, questionnaires

and tests containing 8 questions involving counting problems were applied in two sta-
ges. The application of these instruments was interspersed with workshops to study the

Principle of Drawers and Kaplansky’s Lemmas. The data obtained were analyzed and

discussed, using the theoretical-methodological framework as parameters, where we found

that high school students can appropriate the concepts of the Principle of Drawers and
Kaplansky’s Slogans. However, it is necessary to consolidate the basic concepts of Combi-

natorial Analysis, as some students expressed di(cid:30)culties in understanding these concepts,

which are indispensable in solving such problems. We hope that this study can contri-

bute to the formation and improvement of students and Math teachers in Basic Education.
