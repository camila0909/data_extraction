ABSTRACT

The sets of Julia were named after the French mathematician Gaston Maurice Julia, who
studied such sets and their properties in 1915. These sets are formed by points z in the
complex plane whose orbit by a complex function is limited. In this work, we present a study
about the Julia sets generated by the function fc(z) = z2 + c, where c a complex constant,
called Quadratic Julia Sets. We show that for every c ∈ C we have a set of associated Julia,
which can be a simple ﬁgure as a circle or as a range in the real line, as can be also a fractal,
depending on the constant c chosen. In addition, we also present an algorithm to generate
such sets of Julia using Geogebra software.

Key words: Julia Set. Fractal. Iteration.
