ABSTRACT

In this study, we present the set of the complex numbers, starting from a motivating
problem that suggests a solution by using algebraic third degree equations. In this con-
text, Cardano-Tartaglia ideas naturally show up for solving cubic equations, emphasizing
the insuﬃciency of the real numbers. The complex numbers, thus, impose themselves

aspects, especially concerning their geometric interpretation, namely: addition as vector
sum, multiplication as composition of rotations and homotheties in the complex plan.
Furthermore, the importance of root extraction in C and its relationship with Geometry
became clear.
