Abstract

In this paper, we study a special class of planar curves, convex curves of constant
width. Moreover show basic examples of these curves, starting with the Reuleaux
triangle that indeed is what has the smallest area when the constant width is de(cid:28)ned
unlike the circle has the largest area and is also constant width. We generalize the
construction of the Reuleaux triangle to regular polygons with odd numbers of sides
that are called Reuleaux polygons. Finally we propose activities for the polygons of
study in primary and secondary education as a suggestion for a school education,
by reference to the polygons of Reuleaux in particular the Reuleaux triangle.

Keywords: Polygons; Width; Geometry.
