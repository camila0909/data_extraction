Abstract

The aquatic plants play an important role in the dams, because help in the water

insects. However, some species are spreading and causing great inconvenience to the

of the aquatic macrophyte Eichhornia azurea by inserting the insect predator Thryp-
ticus sp., using mathematical modeling and Markov chains of order 1. For that, a

brief historical of the aquatic plant and of the insect is presented, as well as concepts
related to Markov chains and their applications in biomathematics are discussed, sup-
porting the realization of the modeling of the problem. Actual data obtained from a

survey conducted by the author between 2002 and 2003, in the city of Coxim-MS, were

amount of insects needed to control in each cycle. The use of mathematical modeling

and Markov chains allows the implementation of several tests. Nevertheless, to achieve
more accurate results in relation to biological control we intend, in future work, to use
a predator-prey model and analyze in more detail the problem, add more hypotheses,

and to discuss aspects related to the predator population in environment in question.
The work is a result of the Professional Masters and was written with the intention
that teachers and high school students are being able understand the resolutions of the
examples presented.
