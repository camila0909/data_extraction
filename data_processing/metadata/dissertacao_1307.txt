ABSTRACT

The main objective of this exhibition is to provide the mathematics teacher, given the
high school education, a greater involvement in the study of continuous fractions. Cer-
tain mathematical subjects, generally dealt with in higher education, are far from being
discussed in secondary education. This thesis provides a treatment involving epistemolo-
gical aspects of continuous fractions in order to be objects of study in high school. The
treatment of continuous fractions appears not as a component but as a theme for arti-
culating and signifying the mathematical knowledge present in the current mathematics
curriculum.

Key words: Numerical Sets. Euclidean Algorithm. Continued Fractions. Reduced.
