Abstract

The phenomenon of contagion by the coronavirus, which causes COVID-19, is mathe-

matically modeled. In particular, data relating to the number of cases in the State of

Mato Grosso, Brazil, are used to develop simulations and define the phenomenon. The

model parameters were estimated both from data in the scientific literature and using the

squared parameter adjustment method. Among the simulations, those that estimate the

impact of the population’s adherence or not to measures of social distancing and the use

of masks stand out. One of the results shows that if 70% of the population adhered to

both measures, we would have the contagion extinction in a few months. It is noteworthy

that our research is exploratory in nature and such results, despite being indicative, are

not conclusive. Finally, we observed that the mathematics used to prepare the model

was restricted to the use of difference equations and electronic spreadsheets so that such

model can inspire the use of Mathematical Modeling as a support technique for teaching

Mathematics in High School.

Keywords: Mathematical Modeling, National Curricular Base, Numerical Simulations,

Discrete Mathematical Model.
