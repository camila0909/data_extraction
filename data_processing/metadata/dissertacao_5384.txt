Abstract

This work intends to show that Modular Arithmetic is a current theme and it may be

introduced in high school through a teaching proposal of theme emphasizing on solving

contextualized exercises. For that, initially it presents a theoretical background on the

subject, introduces the Modular Congruence and some of its properties showing the ap-

plication of this concept in solving exercises involving various subjects of mathematics

taken from college entrance examination, Olympics of math and current textbooks. It

makes a brief introduction to the Residuals Classes and presents an application of Modu-

lar Congruence in Cryptography, as well as a report of the proposal application to a high

school class at IFMT, Bela Vista Campus.

Keywords: Modular Arithmetic, Modular Congruence, high school, contextualized exer-

cises, Residuals Classes, Cryptography.
