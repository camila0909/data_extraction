Abstract

In Basic Education, the justiﬁcation given for the study of irrational numbers is based
mainly on the fact that these numbers appear in geometry with formulas to calculate perimeters,
areas and volumes and in algebra to solve equations. In this dissertation, we take a different

not obvious happens because a certain number is irrational. We hope this new perspective that
articulates irrational numbers with problems in arithmetic, geometry and combinatorics would
be helpful for in-service and pre-service teachers of mathematics interested in the teaching and
learning of irrational numbers.
