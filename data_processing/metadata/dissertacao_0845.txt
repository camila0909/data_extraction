ABSTRACT 

The research presented here investigates Bhaskara's contributions to solving 
the  quadratic  equation.  To  this  end,  we  analyze  various  historical  aspects  that 
contributed to the composition of what is now known as the 2nd degree equation or

studied,  initially  permeated  by  the  question  of  whether  Bhaskara  was  the  creator  of 
the formula that bears his name. However, given that numerous scholarly works on 
this  topic  and  mathematical  history  texts  point  out  that  Bhaskara's  creation  of  the

with quadratic equations are remembered. Bhaskara's method, with its own algebraic 
bias, historically produced in India, is considered next. A comparison of the methods 
proposed to recognize Bhaskara's contribution to the construction of a more general 
model  of  quadratic  equation  resolution  model  is  proposed.  The  bibliographical
